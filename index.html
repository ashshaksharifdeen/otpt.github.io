O-TPT

Orthogonality Constraints for Calibrating Test-time Prompt Tuning in Vision-Language Models

Overview Video

Project Overview

Vision-language models suffer from mis-calibration when you fine-tune prompts at test time. O-TPT enforces mutual orthogonality among class-prompt embeddings, yielding both high accuracy and well-calibrated confidences.

Key Contributions

  1. Orthogonality Regularization: Add a term ∥E Eᵀ−I∥₂² to the prompt-tuning loss to enforce angular separation.
  2. Comprehensive Benchmarks: Evaluate on 11 datasets (ImageNet, Fine-grained, OOD variants) with CLIP ViT-B/16 & RN50.
  3. State-of-the-Art Calibration: Achieve 4.21% ECE (vs. 5.13% C-TPT, 12.0% TPT) without sacrificing accuracy.

Methodology

Let E∈ℝ^{C×D} be your matrix of class-prompt embeddings. We optimize: 


minₜ Lₜₚₜ + λ ⋅ ∥E Eᵀ − I_C∥₂²

…where Lₜₚₜ is the standard test-time prompt-tuning loss.
